Derivative

A derivative is the slope of a given equation. Finding a derivative can be useful for finding the rate of change of a given quantity. For example, the derivative or slope of a position versus time graph is velocity at that given time.

To solve a derivative with a given equation, for each value, you must multiply the coefficient by the exponent, then subtract one from the exponent. This means that if you have an exponent of one, the variable(x) will dissapear. If the value is only a constant, than that will dissapear. The derivative also adds a constant to the end of the calculation, which can be determined if given extra information about the equation.

Exmaples

What is the derivative of 3x2 - 9x + 8?

The answer is 6x - 9 + C


What is the derivative of 7x6 + 5x2 + 7x - 9?

The answer is 42x5 + 10x + 7 + C
