Integrals

An Integral is the area under a line or the anti-derivative. Finding an integral is very useful in finding area, as given a certain equation and endpoints, you can find the area under the equation. Integrals are derived from the use of summations. An integral is a summation where the values of the width of the pieces are minicscule. So it is adding a bunch of tiny pieces under the curve of the given equation.

Examples

What is the integral of x2+2?

The answer is x3/3 + 2x


Rules

A Riemann sum is one commonly used way to see how an integral works. Because the pieces are given a defined width, and you pick a point on each block to measure from.
